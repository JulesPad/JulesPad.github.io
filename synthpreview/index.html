The Problem
of fMRI


Everyone should have the option to know if they had a neurodegenerative disease, it is a fundamental right. But unfortunately, this right is not currently entirely available to most people.

The current, most used neuroimaging technology to detect neurogenerative diseases such as Alzheimerâ€™s, MS, and others is fMRI.

This technology is precise but extremely expensive and time-consuming. It leads to immense inequalities in access to these rights. People are generally diagnosed late because of the impossibility of a preemptive diagnosis with fMRI.

In developing countries, access to fMRI is very close to nonexistent, which results in a non-diagnosis of neurogenerative diseases.

These two consequences of the fMRI technology are stopping millions of people every year to exercise their rights for a better treatment and a better life, not only for themselves but also for their families.

Our Vision


Access to quality healthcare

At Synthesia, our vision is to create a future where every person regardless of race, nationality, socioeconomic status, or language,... can access the fundamental right to quality healthcare; more specifically, affordable preventive healthcare.

But also, live a life full of authenticity, not muddled with any brain health anomalies.
